Fibonacci

In mathematics, the Fibonacci numbers, commonly denoted F(n) form a sequence, called the Fibonacci sequence, such that each number is the sum of the two preceding ones, starting from 0 and 1.

This tool calculates F(n) - Fibonacci value for a given positive integer.

Ackermann

In computability theory, the Ackermann function, named after Wilhelm Ackermann, is one of the simplest and earliest-discovered examples of a total computable function that is not primitive recursive.

This tool calculates A(m, n) - Ackermann value for two given positive integers.

Open Ackermann Calculator
Factorial

In mathematics, the factorial of a positive integer n, denoted by n!, is the product of all positive integers less than or equal to n. The symbol for the factorial function is an exclamation mark after a number.

This tool calculates F(n) - Factorial value for a given positive integer.
